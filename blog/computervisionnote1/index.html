Vertical edge detection

Before know how to do the vertical edge detection, we could first know how the convolution operation works in image processing.

we can have a convolution operation like this * means convolution:

$$\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 & 2 & 7 & 4 \\ 1 & 5 & 8 & 9 & 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 7 & 2 & 5 & 1 &3 \\ 0 & 1 & 3 & 1 & 7 & 8 \\ 4 &2&1&6&2&8 \\ 4& 2& 1& 6& 2& 8\\2& 4& 5& 2& 3& 9 \end{bmatrix} * \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 &-1 \\ 1 & 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -5 & -4 & 0 & 8 \\ -10 & -2 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & -2 & -4 & -7 \\ -3 & -2 & -3 & -16 \end{bmatrix} $$

The convolution operation is to do a elements wise multiplication for the fist 3*3 elements to the filter. And them do the next 3*3 filter. For example, the first number -5 is equal to $$3*1+0 * 0+1*-1+ 1 * 1 + 5 * 0 + 8 * -1 + 2 * 1 + 7 * 0 + 2 * -1$$

Why we use the convolution method to detect the vertical edge, let’s have a example of vertical image edges

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 10 &10& 10 &0 &0& 0 \\ 10& 10& 10& 0& 0& 0 \\ 10 &10& 10& 0 &0 &0 \\ 10 &10& 10 &0 &0& 0 \\ 10& 10 &10 &0 &0& 0 \\ 10& 10 &10 &0 &0 &0 \end{bmatrix} * \begin{bmatrix} 1 &0 &-1 \\ 1 &0 &-1 \\ 1 &0 &-1 \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} 0 &30 &30& 0 \\ 0 &30 &30 &0\\ 0& 30 &30 &0 \end{bmatrix}$$

By the convolution operation, we see that in the middle is a number of 30s and that means we find the edges.

Horizontal Edge Detection

For the horizontal edge detection just simply use the filter as the following.

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 &1& 1 \\ 0 &0& 0 \\ -1 &-1& -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

choose a good filter for the edges detection

We already know how to detect the vertical or horizontal edges, now we can discuss some good filter for the edge detection.

$$ Sobel filter :$$

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1& 0& -1 \\ 1 &0 &-2 \\ 1 &0 &-1 \end{bmatrix}$$

$$ Schar filter :$$

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 3 &0 &-3 \\ 10 &0 &-10 \\ 3 &0 &-3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Those filters are for vertical edges if you want to detect horizontal edges, just flip the numbers in the filters.

learn a good filter from complicated images

For example:

$$\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 & 2 & 7 & 4 \\ 1 & 5 & 8 & 9 & 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 7 & 2 & 5 & 1 &3 \\ 0 & 1 & 3 & 1 & 7 & 8 \\ 4 &2&1&6&2&8 \\ 4& 2& 1& 6& 2& 8\\2& 4& 5& 2& 3& 9 \end{bmatrix} * \begin{bmatrix} w1 & w2 &w3 \\ w4 & w5 & w6 \\ w7 & w8 & w9 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -5 & -4 & 0 & 8 \\ -10 & -2 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & -2 & -4 & -7 \\ -3 & -2 & -3 & -16 \end{bmatrix} $$

padding

In previous examples, we found that the image is from 6*6 apply 3*3 filters and end up with 4*4 image output. n*n image apply f*f filter it will end up with output image size:

$$(n-f+1) * (n-f+1)$$

There are two drawbacks. Every time you detect edges you could shrink images and there are a lot overlap in the middle.

padding is the method to solve those two problems.

$$\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 & 2 & 7 & 4 \\ 1 & 5 & 8 & 9 & 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 7 & 2 & 5 & 1 &3 \\ 0 & 1 & 3 & 1 & 7 & 8 \\ 4 &2&1&6&2&8 \\ 4& 2& 1& 6& 2& 8\\2& 4& 5& 2& 3& 9 \end{bmatrix} => \begin{bmatrix}0&0&0&0&0&0&0&0 \\ 0&3 & 0 & 1 & 2 & 7 & 4&0 \\ 0& 1 & 5 & 8 & 9 & 3 & 1&0 \\ 0&2 & 7 & 2 & 5 & 1 &3&0 \\ 0&0 & 1 & 3 & 1 & 7 & 8 &0\\ 0&4 &2&1&6&2&8 &0\\ 0&4& 2& 1& 6& 2& 8&0\\0&2& 4& 5& 2& 3& 9 &0\\0&0&0&0&0&0&0&0 \end{bmatrix} * \begin{bmatrix} 1 &0 &-1 \\ 1 &0 &-1 \\ 1 &0 &-1 \end{bmatrix} $$

For example, first pad the 6*6 image to 8*8 and then apply the filter then you could get the 6*6 images. If padding are added then the size of the image becomes: $$(n+2p-f+1) * (n+2p-f+1)$$

In terms of how many pixel padding to choose, we have some terms ‘valid convolutions’ and ‘same convolutions’. The first one is just no padding, the second means add padding and after the convolutions out put image would be the same as input size.

note: ‘f is usually odd’

striding

Striding is just when you use your filter to conv a image, if stride is 2 you conv jumps by 2 instead of 1. The normal convolution stride is equal to 1.

When we have n*n image and use f*f filter and a stride = s. The output imagesize is equal to $$ \lfloor {(n+2p-f)/s +1} \rfloor * \lfloor {(n+2p-f)/s +1} \rfloor$$

summary of convolutions

Convolution in math textbook is different !!!

We need to flip filter apply the convolutions

For examples $$ \begin{bmatrix} 3 &4 &5 \\ 1 &0 &2 \\ -1 &9 &7 \end{bmatrix} => \begin{bmatrix} 7 &2 &5 \\ 9 &0 &4 \\ -1 &1 &3 \end{bmatrix}$$

In computer science we just skipped the flip operation, this operation is called cross-correlation.
